Skipping Numbers in the Power Series

Typically, we define a power series as \[ \sum_{0}^{\infty{}}a_{n}x^{n} \] The problem I had come across recently was of the form \( a_{n} = \frac{1}{(kn)!} \) where \( k \in{} \mathbb{N} \). Of course, somewhat trivially, for \( k=1 \), \[ \sum_{0}^{\infty{}}\frac{x^{n}}{n!} = e^{x} \] But what about \( k = 2, 3, ...\)? Well, it turns out that the answer is pretty simple.
